CAD Module - 7 Days

Day 1: Introduction to CAD & Basic Modeling

Overview:
Begin by understanding what CAD is and why it is a vital skill for engineers and designers. Learn about different types of CAD systems and get familiar with the design environment.

Key Learnings:

Importance of CAD in engineering, product design, and manufacturing.
Types of CAD: 2D drafting (for technical drawings) and 3D modeling (for realistic digital prototypes).
Popular software: AutoCAD, SolidWorks, Fusion 360, and when to use each.
Exploring the CAD workspace: menus, toolbars, command line, and drawing area.
Basic drawing commands: Line, Circle, Rectangle, Arc and how to combine them to create simple sketches.

Learn to create precise sketches and transform them into 3D features.

Key Learnings:

Applying geometric constraints (parallel, perpendicular, tangent) to control shapes.
Introduction to core 3D features: Extrude, Revolve, Sweep, Loft to turn sketches into solids.
Modifying geometry with Fillet, Chamfer, and Shell for smoother edges and lightweight structures.
Understanding the feature tree/history for organized design changes.

Overview:

Key Learnings:

Adding dimensions (linear, angular, radius) to define size and relationships.
